Vanillekipferl



These scrumptious crescent shaped cookies are from Austria, Hungary and East Europe. They're made at the beginning of Advent in Germany, four Sundays before Christmas. Traditionally made with almond flour and served with a good dusting of vanilla sugar, they go down mighty fine with a cup of coffee, or just by themselves!

Chocolate Chip Cookies



An all-American classic, the chocolate chip cookie was invented by Ruth Wakefield in the 1930s. She called them Tollhouse Chocolate Crunch Cookies, named after her speciality restaurant. The Bakeaway's cookies are crisp on the outside, and soft and chewy on the inside. They make a delightful accompaniment to coffee, or even as dessert!
